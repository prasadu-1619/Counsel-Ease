Introduction

Depression is a common but serious mental health condition that can significantly impact your daily life, relationships, and overall well-being. While it s normal to feel down or sad sometimes, depression goes beyond temporary emotional states and persists for longer periods, affecting various aspects of your life.

Risk factors

The risk factors for depression could be medical, biochemical or genetic, social or even circumstantial. Common risk factors include:
  • Sex:The prevalence of major depression is twice in females than in males.
  • Genetics:There is a higher risk of depression when you have a family history of depression.
  • Social class: The socioeconomic status of a person, financial difficulties and a perception of low social standing could increase the risk of depression.
  • Certain medicines: Certain medications, including certain types of hormone birth control pills, corticosteroids and beta-blockers are associated with a greater likelihood of depression.
  • Vitamin D deficiency: Studies have linked depressive symptoms to low levels vitamin D.
  • Gender identity: Depression risk among transgender individuals is almost four times as that of people who are cisgender according to a study from 2018.
  • Use of drugs: Around 21 percent of those with a substance abuse disorder also suffer from depression.
  • Medical conditions: Depression is often associated with chronic medical conditions. Heart disease sufferers are approximately twice as likely to suffer from depression as people with no heart disease, while one in four cancer patients could have depression as well.

Types of Depression

Depression is a broad dysfunctional behavior. Presents with discouraged disposition, loss of certainty or absence of interest in delight, diminished energy, sentiments of regret or low energy, Self-worth, rest, or craving disturbed and powerless fixations. Furthermore, depression likewise happens with indications of tension. These issues might be gotten constant or repetitive and lead to huge debilitation of the person's ability to deal with their everyday obligations.

Atypical depression- In typical mild, moderate, or severe depressive episodes, the patient suffers from lowering of mood, reduction of energy, and decrease in activity. Capacity for enjoyment, interest, and concentration is reduced, and marked tiredness after even minimum effort is common. Sleep is usually disturbed and appetite diminished. Self-esteem and self-confidence are almost always reduced and, even in the mild form, some ideas of guilt or worthlessness are often present. The lowered mood varies little from day to day, is unresponsive to circumstances and may be accompanied by so-called "somatic" symptoms, such as loss of interest and pleasurable feelings, waking in the morning several hours before the usual time, depression worst in the morning, marked psychomotor retardation, agitation, loss of appetite, weight loss, and loss of libido. Depending upon the number and severity of the symptoms, a depressive episode may be specified as mild, moderate or severe.

Recurrent depressive disorder (RDD)- A disorder characterized by repeated episodes of depression as described for depressive episode, without any history of independent episodes of mood elevation and increased energy. There may, however, be brief episodes of mild mood elevation and overactivity immediately after a depressive episode, sometimes precipitated by antidepressant treatment. The more severe forms of recurrent depressive disorder have much in common with earlier concepts such as manic-depressive depression, melancholia, vital depression and endogenous depression. The first episode may occur at any age from childhood to old age, the onset may be either acute or insidious, and the duration varies from a few weeks to many months.

Psychotic depression or Depression with psychotic symptoms- An episode of depression, but with the presence of hallucinations, delusions, psychomotor retardation, or stupor so severe that ordinary social activities are impossible; there may be danger to life from suicide, dehydration, or starvation. The hallucinations and delusions may or may not be mood-congruent.

Perinatal/Post-partum Depression- People who just had pregnancy/post-labor face this sort of depression. Researches now suggest that even males can suffer from a diagnosis of perinatal or post-partum depression. An individual confronting this sort of depression must accept depression counselling from a reliable depression therapist as an initial step.
